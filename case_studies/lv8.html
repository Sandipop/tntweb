Ux Case Study

Problem Statement

Physical rehabilitation is often inaccessible, time-consuming, and inconsistent in feedback. Patients struggle to stay motivated and follow correct form during home exercises, while physiotherapists lack real-time progress insights.

Objectives & Goals

  • Make physiotherapy accessible anywhere without special equipment.
  • Provide real-time guidance and feedback using AI motion tracking.
  • Enable physiotherapists to monitor, track, and personalize recovery programs remotely.
  • Give admins a comprehensive dashboard to manage users and operations.

Design a seamless digital physiotherapy system that connects patients, physiotherapists, and administrators in one ecosystem — promoting AI-driven recovery, real-time motion tracking, and personalized rehabilitation.

Observations

50%

Physiotherapists mentioned spending a significant amount of time manually tracking patient progress and logs.

80%

Patients reported difficulty remembering daily exercise instructions without visual or guided support, leading to inconsistency in recovery.

30%

Patients admitted skipping sessions because they lacked progress visibility and motivation during long-term recovery processes.

90%

Physiotherapists stated that having a digital tool to assign exercises, track pain levels and monitor progress would streamline their workflow.

60%

Patients said they would feel more motivated if they could track recovery visually and receive reminders from their therapist.

User Persona

Description

Aarav is a software engineer recovering from a lower back muscle strain. He struggles to maintain a consistent physiotherapy routine due to tight work schedules. He prefers quick, guided exercises and digital progress tracking to stay motivated and ensure proper form.

A day in their life

  • Logs in and checks his assigned physiotherapy exercises before work hours.
  • Spends most of the day sitting at a workstation, causing mild discomfort.
  • Performs guided exercises and tracks pain levels in the evening.
  • Connects with his physiotherapist for corrections or routine adjustments.

Pain points

  • Finds it difficult to remember exercise steps without visual reference.
  • Fails to maintain consistency due to workload and lack of reminders.
  • Performs guided exercises and tracks pain levels in the evening.
  • Unsure whether he is performing movements correctly or causing strain.
